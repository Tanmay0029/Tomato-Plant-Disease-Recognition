About



This model is developed used for detecting the diseases which can occur on tomato plants using the tomato leaf images.

Details about the model


  • Technology used: Convolution Neural Networks
  • Accuracy: Correct Prediction given 98 times out of 100
  • Dataset used: Labelled Tomato Leaf images
  • Dataset source kaggle
  • Programming Language Python
  • Model ResNet152V2

The AI model developed for tomato leaf disease prediction utilizes the powerful Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) algorithm, specifically leveraging the ResNet152V2 architecture. This model is designed to take images of tomato leaves as input and analyze them to determine whether the leaves are healthy or affected by a specific disease. The ResNet152V2, a refined version of the original ResNet model, offers significant improvements in image recognition tasks by addressing the vanishing gradient problem and allowing for deeper network training. Its ability to learn intricate patterns and features from the input images makes it an ideal choice for this application.

By training the model on a comprehensive dataset of tomato leaf images, both healthy and diseased, the AI system becomes adept at recognizing various disease symptoms. The CNN algorithm processes the input images through multiple layers of convolution, pooling, and activation functions, extracting relevant features that indicate the presence of diseases such as early blight, late blight, and leaf mold, among others. The final output layer of the ResNet152V2 model provides a classification result, indicating whether the leaf is healthy or specifying the type of disease present. This AI-driven approach not only enhances the accuracy and speed of disease detection but also offers a scalable solution for farmers and agricultural experts to monitor and maintain the health of their tomato crops more effectively.
